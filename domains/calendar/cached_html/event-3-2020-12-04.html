Location

Those who want to carpool or caravan, meet at 7:00AM inCoors Tek Parking lot, east side of the American Mountaineering Bldg in Golden. Park on the south end next to the Golden Solar Building. Here's a link to it in Google Maps: (https://goo.gl/maps/VLqhSXXK5D52 ).

For those who want to meet us at the trailhead, meet us atthe Ole Barn Knoll Trailhead in Golden Gate Canyon State Park. , about mile inside the park from West Entry to Castlewood Canyon State Park. Here is a Google Maps Link to the trailhead: https://goo.gl/maps/RGpWb8vRLgCmeL5T9Meet us at the trailhead at 7:45am. There are restrooms at the trailhead.

For more info Contact

Jim Guerra, 720-320-7262, joguerra77062@gmail.com

Details

We will start and end our hike at the Ole Barn Knoll Trailhead. Heres a Google Maps link to the trailhead: https://goo.gl/maps/RGpWb8vRLgCmeL5T9



From the Ole Barn Knoll Trailhead, we will hike the Mule Deer Loop Trail clockwise. Trail conditions permitting, we will also take a side trip to the highest point on Mt. Thorodin, where we will be rewarded with great views of the Indian Peaks and Longs Peak / Mt Meeker.



If this Trailhead, or the Mountain Base Road, is closed, we will start and end this hike at Panorama Point instead (Google Maps link: https://goo.gl/maps/ECGf4GYubcuVAFKu7 ).



Given the time of year, you should bring your microspikes. If trail conditions warrant snowshoes, we will still do the Mule Deer Loop Trail, but probably not go up Mt. Thorodin.

Notes

The Bobcats mission is to provide a variety of challenging CMC trips, mostly on weekdays, for our mostly age 50+ constituents. We dont have dues or elections and we invite all CMC members to sign up for any Bobcat trip. We send out a monthly email with all the coming months trips just after they are activated. You can also search the CMC Calendar for Bobcats trips any time by using an asterisk ( * ) in the Keyword Search box. To join our free mailing list and become a Bobcat member, write or call CMC Membership Services at office@cmc.org or (303)-279-3080.

The latest COVID Hiking Protocolscan be found at: https://www.cmc.org/Portals/0/Documents/COVID-19%20Protocol%20for%20CMC%20Trips%20%281%29.pdf
