Location

Meet in Buffalo Creek at the USFS Work Center on Highway 126 at 8:30 am. We will then drive to the trailhead which is about 2 miles further on 126. Carpooling from Buffalo Creek to the trailhead is entirely optional, but for anyone who iscomfortable doing so for that short distance that will help as the parking at the trailhead is limited.

For more info Contact

John Mill

Cell 303-601-5635

Email jmill1460@gmail.com

Details

This is a great late season hike. Part of the hike will be on a Forest Service road, and some will be off trail. If conditions permit, the majority will be off trail. We will hike through part of the area that burned in the Buffalo Creek Fire in 1996. Some of the off trail will involve up and down as we navigate cross-country through the burn area. Deer and elk are frequently seen in this area. The last 400' or so of elevation gain to get to the top of Raleigh Peak is a little bit steep and can be slippery if there is snow. Scrambling up the final summit boulders is optional. Great views from the top for miles.

Notes

Bring warm layers,and be prepared for wind and possible rain or snow.
